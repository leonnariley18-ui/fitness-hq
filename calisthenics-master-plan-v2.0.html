Two progressive strength systems running in parallel. Push-ups (upper body) and pistol squats (lower body) alternate — so you're always either building or recovering, but never both at once. Same 2-week block system, same recovery rules, completely different muscle groups.

How the Block System Works
Structure
Each progression = 4 blocks of 2 weeks each. Each block = 6 sessions (3x per week). Complete one block, come back to Claude to generate the next one.
Why 3x Per Week
Muscle growth happens during REST, not training. 48-72 hour recovery between sessions = body repairs stronger. More ≠ better. Rest days are when you actually get stronger.
Push-Up Progression (Upper Body)

Goal: Full floor push-ups. 8-week program, four 2-week blocks. Each session: Quick - LEG warm-up → push-ups → post-push-up stretches (4 min). Total: 16-22 min.

Push-Up Blocks

Warm-up: Quick - LEG (~90 sec) · Cool-down: post-push-up stretch (4 min)

Universal Form Guidelines

  • Hands slightly wider than shoulders, weight distributed across entire palm
  • Elbows at 45° from body — "arrow" shape, not "T" shape
  • Core engaged — entire body one rigid plank, no sagging hips
  • Inhale on the way down, exhale on the way up. Never hold your breath.
  • Lower until chest nearly touches surface, push back to full extension
  • Scapular push-ups always before plank hold to preserve form quality
Pistol Squat Progression (Lower Body)

Goal: Full unassisted pistol squat. Runs on push-up REST days. Each session: SFB or Quick + LEG warm-up → pistol squat session → post-pistol squat stretches (4 min). Total: ~25 min. Equipment: bodyweight, wall, chair, yoga blocks, jade mat.
