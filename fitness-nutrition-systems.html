The Situation
  • Baseline: ~1200-1400 cal/day average (May 2026)
  • Weekdays structured by work, weekends = 400-500 cal days
  • Grazer personality: 10 small eating occasions vs. 3 big meals
  • Fast metabolism: hungry again within 3 hours
The Goal
  • Cosmetic weight gain: more curves, fill out frame
  • Doctor declined cyproheptadine prescription (too healthy)
  • Build sustainable eating habits WITHOUT appetite stimulant
  • Work WITH grazer personality, not against it
Core Problem Identified
Brain categorizes eating as an INTERRUPTION instead of FUEL. When busy, food feels like a task that competes with productivity rather than the thing that enables it. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle: eat less → feel less hungry → eat even less. The solution isn't forcing bigger meals — it's making frequent small eating occasions COUNT more calorically.
Step 1: Strategic Snack Upgrade (May-June 2026)

Timeline: 2-3 weeks (through early June). Next check-in: week of June 2nd. The goal is to add 500-800 calories per day through upgraded snacking. Zero behavior change — just making existing snacks count more.

Weekend 4-Phase Strategy
Designed for bed rotting lifestyle. Goal: NOT another 400-calorie Saturday. Even hitting 1200-1400 on weekends is a huge win.
Phase 1 (wake up 1-2pm): Bedside snacks (peanut M&Ms, jerky, nuts) = 200-300 cal | Phase 2 (3-5pm): One real eating occasion (order food, grab leftovers) = 400-600 cal | Phase 3 (7pm-midnight): Evening grazing with good snacks = 300-500 cal | Phase 4 (optional late night): If hungry, don't ignore it = 200-400 cal
Friday Night Setup Routine
Every Friday before bed: (1) Refill bedside container with peanut M&Ms, jerky, nuts. (2) Grab 3-4 string cheese from fridge, put in room for Saturday. (3) Check snacks stocked for weekend. This 3-minute setup = difference between 400-cal Saturday and 1400-cal Saturday.

Tracking System (Simplified)

Weekdays: quick check (3 data points). Weekends: detailed food diary with photos. Designed to avoid making eating feel like a job.

Weekday Check (Daily)
  • Snacks eaten today: yes/no or brief list
  • Daily vibe: good/okay/bad (based on how body FELT, not performance)
  • Notes (optional): anything worth mentioning
Weekend Log (Detailed)
  • Full food diary with times, hunger levels, photos
  • Rough calorie estimate for the day
  • Weekend-specific challenges and wins
What "Daily Vibe" Means
This is a BODY CHECK, not a performance grade. Good day = decent energy, didn't feel weak/shitty. Okay day = fine but not great, maybe low energy. Bad day = felt physically bad, low energy, brain fog. If low energy is hormonal (period, bad sleep, stress), note that separately so patterns are clear.
