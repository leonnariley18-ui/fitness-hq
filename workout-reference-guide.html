Yoga Strap Flexibility Routine

12-14 minutes  ·  always last in any session  ·  3-4x per week  ·  yoga strap, belt, towel, or resistance band

1. Standing Forward Fold
2 minutes
Feet hip-width, soft bend in knees. Hinge forward and hang. Hold opposite elbows or let arms dangle. Don't force — let gravity do the work.
You'll feel
Hamstrings, calves, lower back decompressing.
Breathing
On each exhale, get heavier. Don't bounce or force.
2. Supine Hamstring Stretch
4 min (2 min each leg)
Lie on back. Loop strap around ball of right foot. Extend leg toward ceiling. Draw leg toward you gently. Every 30 sec exhale and draw a little closer. Switch legs.
You'll feel
Deep hamstring and calf — more than expected.
Modification
Bend knee slightly if very tight — stretch is still there.
3. Supine Hip Opener (Figure Four)
4 min (2 min each side)
Cross right ankle over left thigh. Loop strap around left foot, extend toward ceiling. Draw both legs toward you. Slow steady breaths. Switch sides.
You'll feel
Deep outer hip and glute.
Modification
Keep bottom foot on floor if strap feels like too much.
4. Shoulder and Chest Opener
1 minute
Stand or sit. Hold strap behind back wider than shoulder-width, arms straight. On each inhale lift arms slightly behind and away from your body.
Why it matters
Counters all the forward pressing from push-ups. Front of shoulders, chest, biceps.
5. Overhead Side Stretch
1 min (30 sec each side)
Hold strap overhead wider than shoulder-width, taut. Lean gently to one side, hold, breathe, switch. Both arms stay straight.
You'll feel
Lats, side body, obliques, shoulders.
6. Seated Forward Fold
2 minutes
Legs extended, loop strap around both feet. Hinge from hips on each exhale, spine long. Walk hands further down the strap every 30 sec as your body softens.
Key
Length through the spine, not nose to knees. By now hamstrings have been working 8+ minutes — this fold will go deeper.

Why 2-minute holds work

This is where connective tissue actually adapts, not just feels good temporarily. Combined with progressive deepening you're training your nervous system to release. Do this 3-4x a week consistently and you will get more flexible. Patience is the whole game.

Targeted Practices

All versions: SFB or Quick + LEG warm-up  ·  120 sec per pose  ·  no cool-down needed  ·  ~15-22 min total

TP V1
Hips, Splits and Grounding
Post-Arm-Day
  • Low Lunge — hip flexors, front of back thigh
  • Half Split — hamstrings, back of front thigh
  • Warrior I — hip flexors, chest, shoulders
  • Butterfly Pose — inner groin, hips
  • Supine Twist — spinal rotation, outer hips
Best after: push-up sessions or any upper body day. Keeps hips open and counteracts sitting.
TP V2
Spine, Hips and Twist
Post-Leg-Day
  • Melting Heart — thoracic spine, shoulders, chest
  • Seated Spinal Twist — spinal rotation, outer hips
  • Reclined Pigeon — deep outer hip, glute
  • Butterfly Pose — inner groin, hips
  • Supine Twist — spinal rotation, outer hips
Best after: pistol squat sessions or any lower body day. Decompress the legs and spine after heavy leg work.
TP V3
Deep Hip Open and Balance
Solo Practice
  • Pigeon Pose — deep outer hip, glute, hip flexor
  • Half Split — hamstrings, back of front thigh
  • Warrior III — balance, hamstrings, glutes, core
  • Butterfly Pose — inner groin, hips
  • Supine Twist — spinal rotation, outer hips
Best as: standalone practice or on rest days. The most challenging of the three — good for when you want something meaningful without a full workout.
