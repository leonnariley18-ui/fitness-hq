Yoga Strap Flexibility Routine

Block 1 Reward — Unlocked March 13, 2026
12-14 minutes  ·  3-4x per week  ·  always last in any session
What you need
Yoga strap (or belt, towel, resistance band)
Duration
12-14 minutes
Frequency
3-4x per week — this is where real gains happen
Vibe
Slow, intentional, progressive. Goal: actually get bendier.
The Routine
1. Standing Forward Fold
2 minutes
Stand with feet hip-width apart, soft bend in the knees. Hinge forward from your hips and let your upper body hang completely. Hold opposite elbows or let arms dangle. Don't force your head down — let gravity do the work.
You'll feel
Hamstrings, calves, lower back decompressing.
Breathing
On each exhale, let yourself get heavier. Don't bounce or force.
2. Supine Hamstring Stretch
4 minutes (2 min each leg)
Lie on your back. Loop strap around the ball of your right foot. Extend leg toward the ceiling, as straight as comfortable. Hold strap with both hands and gently draw the leg toward you. Switch legs.
You'll feel
Deep hamstring and calf — more than you expect after the fold warmed things up.
Breathing
Every 30 sec, exhale and draw the leg a little closer. Progressive deepening.
Modification
Bend the knee slightly if hamstrings are very tight — the stretch is still there.
3. Supine Hip Opener (Figure Four)
4 minutes (2 min each side)
Still on your back. Cross right ankle over left thigh (figure four). Loop strap around left foot and extend that leg toward the ceiling. Draw both legs gently toward you. Switch sides.
You'll feel
Deep into the outer hip and glute — exactly the area yoga flows flag.
Breathing
This one is intense. Slow, steady breaths. Don't hold your breath.
Modification
Keep bottom foot on the floor if the strap feels like too much — stretch is still there.
4. Shoulder & Chest Opener
1 minute
Stand or sit cross-legged. Hold strap behind your back wider than shoulder-width, arms straight. On each inhale, lift arms slightly behind and away from your body.
You'll feel
Front of shoulders, chest, biceps.
Why it matters
Counters all the forward pressing from push-ups. Your shoulders will thank you.
5. Overhead Side Stretch
1 minute (30 sec each side)
Standing or seated. Hold strap overhead wider than shoulder-width, keeping it taut. Lean gently to one side. Hold, breathe, switch sides.
You'll feel
Lats, side body, obliques, shoulders.
Key
Both arms stay straight — don't bend the elbow on the leaning side.
6. Seated Forward Fold
2 minutes
Sit on the floor, legs extended. Loop strap around both feet. Sit tall — on each exhale, hinge forward from the hips (not the waist), spine long. Every 30 sec, walk hands further down the strap as your body softens.
You'll feel
Hamstrings, calves, lower back — the full posterior chain finish.
Key
Length through the spine, not nose to knees. By now hamstrings have been working 8+ minutes — this fold will go deeper.
Why This Works

The Science of 2-Minute Holds

Holding 2 minutes per side instead of 1 is where connective tissue starts to genuinely adapt — not just feel good temporarily. Combined with progressive deepening (exhaling further into each stretch), you're training your nervous system to release rather than just sitting in the same position.

Do this 3-4x a week consistently and you will get more flexible. It just takes time. Patience is the whole game.

When to Use
  • After push-up sessions (always last)
  • After pistol squat sessions (always last)
  • After yoga flows (always last)
  • Standalone on rest days — 12 minutes is nothing
  • After targeted practice sessions (always last)
